Oilseed rape (Brassica napus L.) was formed ~7500 years ago by hybridization between B. rapa and B. oleracea, followed by chromosome doubling, a process known as allopolyploidy. Together with more ancient polyploidizations, this conferred an aggregate 72Ã— genome multiplication since the origin of angiosperms and high gene content.

The final assembly of 849.7 Mb was obtained using SOAP and Newbler, with 89% nongapped sequence. The assembly covers ~79% of the 1130-Mb genome and includes 95.6% of Brassica expressed sequence tags (ESTs). A single-nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) map genetically anchored 712.3 Mb (84%) of the genome assembly, yielding pseudomolecules for the 19 chromosomes.
