About Populus trichocarpa

Populus trichocarpa (poplar) is a deciduous broadleaf tree that is native to Western North America. It is an economically important source of timber. Its rapid growth and compact genome size (~500 Mbp, n=18) has lead to its use as a model organism for tree species.

Assembly

The genome assembly of black cottonwood was published in 2006. Sequencing by the US department of Energy Joint Genome Institute (JGI) followed a whole genome shotgun strategy reaching 7.5x coverage, with scaffolds, where possible, being assigned to the genetic map.

The assembly was constructed with Arachne (version 20071016HA) with an attempt to merge the outbred haplotypes and an extensive attempt to remove contaminating sequence. The latest genetic mapping information was integrated to construct the 19 chromosome size scaffold. The total length of assembled sequences is 403 Mbp.

Annotation

Gene predictions, by JGI, Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL) and University of Ghent, combined 'known' poplar genes, protein homology-based and ab initio gene predictions using the JGI annotation pipeline.

Regulation

  • Probes from the Poplar Genome Array Affymetrix GeneChip were aligned to the genome using the Ensembl Functional Genomics pipeline.
